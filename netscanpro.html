Why It's Different

It doesn't just scan.
It teaches.

Most network scanners hand you a wall of port numbers and walk away. NetScanPro explains them in plain language so you actually understand what you're looking at.

Tap port 445 and you'll see SMB — file sharing — flagged with a security note explaining the WannaCry ransomware connection and why exposing it on an untrusted network is risky. Tap port 3389 and you'll see RDP, with a note about brute-force attempts on internet-facing hosts. Every scanned port comes with context: what the service does, what it's for, and what to watch out for.

Network printers, IP cameras, RDP, Telnet, MQTT — services that are harmless on your home Wi-Fi but a problem if your network is misconfigured. NetScanPro flags them.

It's built for the curious — people who want to know what's actually running on their network, not just see a count.

FAQ

Common questions.

Is this like Fing?

Similar device discovery, but no subscriptions and no servers. Fewer bells and whistles — more privacy. NetScanPro doesn't phone home, doesn't require an account, and unlocks fully for a one-time $6.99.

Will it work on my home network?

Yes — any standard home Wi-Fi. NetScanPro is designed for /24 IPv4 subnets and is IPv6-aware. Works on Wi-Fi 5, 6, 6E, and 7 routers from any vendor.

Why does it ask for local network permission?

iOS requires that permission for any app that scans the local network. It's the same prompt Sonos, Hue, and AirPlay apps trigger. Explained in the App Store privacy disclosure.

Why doesn't it find my Alexa?

Amazon devices use proprietary discovery protocols that don't broadcast on standard mDNS or respond to typical TCP probes. We're working on better detection for a future release.

Do you sell my data?

No. There's nothing to sell. Nothing leaves your device — no scan history, no device list, no analytics. There's no server to leak from because there's no server.

What happens if I switch phones?

Tap "Restore Purchase" inside NetScanPro on the new device. Your Pro entitlement follows your Apple ID, so any iPhone or iPad signed in gets Pro features automatically.
