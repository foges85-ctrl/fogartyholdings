Why It's Different

Documentation happens at the
worst possible time.

The moment worth documenting never waits for a convenient one. A student blows up mid-lesson. The same kid asks for a third bathroom pass before lunch. Something happens that you know you'll need a record of — and you're standing in front of thirty students with zero time to write it down.

So it goes on a sticky note. Or into your phone, which looks terrible in front of a class. Or — most often — it gets reconstructed from memory at four o'clock: vague, undated, and hard to stand behind when it matters.

PrepPeriod splits the job in two. Capture takes two taps on your Apple Watch, without interrupting class or pulling out your phone — and every entry is timestamped the moment it happens. Complete happens later: during your prep period, the app reminds you to add the details while they're still fresh.

The result is a clear, credible, timestamped record — the kind that holds up in parent conferences, IEP meetings, and conversations with administrators.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do I need my phone in class?

No — that's the point. Capture happens entirely on your Apple Watch, in two taps, without pulling out your phone or turning away from your class. Your phone can stay in the drawer where your school probably wants it anyway.

What can I log from the watch?

Behavior incidents, positive notes, hall passes (bathroom, water, nurse), parent-contact reminders, and attendance. Each capture takes two taps and is timestamped the moment you make it.

What happens during my prep period?

PrepPeriod reminds you to complete the entries you captured during class. Each one already has the who, what, and exactly when — you just add the details while they're still fresh.

Can I use it without an Apple Watch?

Yes. Everything works on iPhone too — capture, completion, attendance, and reports. The watch is what makes in-the-moment capture effortless, but it isn't required.

Where is student data stored?

On your own devices, synced privately through your personal iCloud account. There are no accounts to create and no servers involved — Fogarty Holdings never sees or stores your classroom records.

Does it handle rotating or A/B schedules?

Yes. Set up your rotation once and PrepPeriod knows which classes meet on which day — attendance and capture always show the right roster for today.

What are the PDF reports for?

Parent conferences, IEP meetings, and administrator discussions. A report pulls a student's full timestamped history — incidents, positives, passes, parent contacts — into a clean, printable document you can stand behind.

Is there a subscription?

No. PrepPeriod is free to download and use every day. Professional PDF reports are a single one-time unlock, handled entirely by Apple — pay once and it's yours on every device signed into your Apple ID.
