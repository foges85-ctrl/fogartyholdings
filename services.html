FAQ

Common Questions

Will I actually own the app? +

Yes — completely. For iOS apps, you sign up for your own Apple Developer account ($99/year) before development begins, and the app is published under your name from day one. App Store revenue flows directly to your bank account. For websites, the source code is transferred to your private GitHub repo, deployed to hosting under your account. All custom code becomes your property upon final payment.

How long does an iOS app take? +

It depends entirely on scope, which is exactly why we start with a free discovery call. After our conversation I'll provide a written estimate with an hour range. Calendar time also depends on App Store review, which can take 1–7 days per submission. Simple, single-purpose utility apps tend to be the sweet spot.

What happens if my project goes over the estimate? +

I check in with you before approaching the upper end of the estimate. If new requirements emerge mid-build, we go through a written change order before any extra hours are billed. You will never get a surprise invoice.

What kinds of projects don't you take on? +

I focus on what I can deliver well: single-purpose utility apps and small-to-medium static websites. I don't take on enterprise projects, anything requiring custom backend infrastructure I'd need to maintain long-term, or projects with regulatory complexity (HIPAA, financial services, etc.).

Do you offer support after launch? +

Yes — through monthly maintenance retainers at the same $100/hour rate. Choose a block of hours per month (typically 2 or 5) for bug fixes, content updates, iOS compatibility patches, and App Store resubmissions. Hours don't roll over, so you only pay for what you actually need.

How do payments work? +

25% deposit upfront against the estimated hours, applied to your final invoice. After that, I bill weekly with itemized hour logs so you can see exactly what was worked on. Payment via Stripe or direct invoice. All work is governed by a written client agreement covering scope, IP transfer, and payment terms.
